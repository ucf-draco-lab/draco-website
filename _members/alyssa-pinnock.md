Alyssa Pinnock is a senior Computer Engineering student at the University of Central Florida, specializing in the Digital VLSI Circuits track. She worked as a Digital Firmware Intern at Northrop Grumman in Summer 2025, where she gained hands-on experience in FPGA and digital firmware workflows. Alyssa has also conducted undergraduate research with the Florida Space Institute and is looking forward to expanding her interests in microarchitecture, verification, and hardware security with the DRACO Lab.

Outside of research, Alyssa enjoys staying involved on campus as a Treasurer for Women in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (WEECS) and a member of the Society of Women Engineers (SWE). In her free time, Alyssa enjoys reading, playing video games, and trying out new foods.
